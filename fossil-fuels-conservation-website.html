Understanding Fossil Fuels

Oil

Petroleum products power transportation and are used in thousands of everyday items. Global reserves are limited, and extraction causes significant environmental damage.

Natural Gas

The cleanest burning fossil fuel, natural gas is used for heating, electricity, and industrial processes. While abundant, it still contributes to greenhouse gas emissions.

Coal

Long used for electricity generation and industrial processes, coal is the most carbon-intensive fossil fuel and a major contributor to climate change and air pollution.

Fossil fuels are non-renewable resources formed over millions of years from ancient organic matter. Once depleted, they cannot be replaced within human timescales. Additionally, their combustion releases greenhouse gases that contribute to climate change and air pollution that affects human health.

Conservation of these resources is crucial for:

  • Extending the availability of these finite resources
  • Reducing environmental impacts including climate change
  • Improving air quality and public health
  • Providing time for transition to renewable energy sources
  • Ensuring energy security for future generations

How You Can Help Conserve Fossil Fuels

1 Transportation

  • Use public transportation when possible
  • Carpool or rideshare for commuting
  • Consider electric or hybrid vehicles
  • Walk or bike for short distances
  • Maintain your vehicle for optimal efficiency

2 Home Energy

  • Improve home insulation and weatherproofing
  • Use energy-efficient appliances
  • Install programmable thermostats
  • Switch to LED lighting
  • Consider solar panels or renewable energy options

3 Daily Habits

  • Turn off lights and electronics when not in use
  • Reduce hot water usage
  • Air-dry clothes when possible
  • Buy local products to reduce transportation emissions
  • Choose products with minimal packaging

4 Advocacy

  • Support renewable energy policies
  • Encourage your workplace to adopt green practices
  • Participate in community conservation programs
  • Educate others about fossil fuel conservation
  • Support research and development of alternative energies
